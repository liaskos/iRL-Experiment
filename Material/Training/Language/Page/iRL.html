iStar-RL: Entities and Relationships "cheat sheet"

Entities

Entity
 Definition
 Examples
Active, autonomous entity that holds beliefs and aims at achieving goals. Includes humans, machines (e.g., AI), and organizations thereof. “Inventory Manager”, “Student”, “Travel agency”, “Auto Pilot”, “Canada Revenue Agency”

A state of affairs that an actor wants to achieve
 “Have Medical Supplies Replenished”, “Have Invoice Paid”, “Get House Cleaned”, “Have Car Repaired”

An attempt to an action by an actor, usually with the purpose of achieving some goal
 “Order Supplies Online”, “Purchase Supplies from Store”, “Complete Homework Assignment”, “Complete Homework Assignment”, “Write Exam”

An outcome of an action attempt (task) that constitutes successful execution of the action
 “Order Successfully Received”, “Exam Passed with High Grade”, “Exam Passed with OK Grade”

An outcome of an action attempt (task) that constitutes failed execution of the action
 “Order Did Not Register”, “Email Failed to Be Sent”, “Participation to Exam was Not Possible”, “Collision was Not Averted”

An outcome that is implied by one or more other effects
 “Stock Is Below Reorder Point (after a shipment)”, “Eligible for Graduation/Schollarship (after passing a course)”, “Account Balance below $1,000 (after a withdrawal)”, “Registration Site Open (after enabling it)”

The outcome of the previous action attempt
 “Stock Was Below Reorder Point (before a shipment)”, “Was Eligible for Graduation/Schollarship (before taking a course)”, “Account Balance was below $1,000 (before a withdrawal)”, “Registration Site Was Open (before attempting to enable it)”

An attribute for which an actor desires some level of achievement
 “Expedite Delivery”, “Minimize Costs”, “Safeguard Privacy”, “Avoid Risks”

The level of achievement of a quality before the latest action
 “Expedite Delivery (before a delivery)”, “Minimize Costs (before a purchase)”, “Safeguard Privacy (before sharing information)”, “Avoid Risks (before purchasing an investment”


Relationships

Relationship
 Definition
 Examples

(wants relationship)

 An agent wants to achieve a specific goal
  • “John wants-to have house cleaned”
  • “President wants-to have policy announced”,
  • “Contractor wants-to get renovation project completed”

(AND refinment)

 A goal is decomposed into multiple tasks, performance of all of which is necessary for the fulfillment of the parent goal
  • “[Have Groceries Purchased] is AND-refined to [Go to the Store], [Place Items in Cart], [Pay for Items]”
  • “[Have Course Requirements Fulfilled] is AND-refined to [Enroll Course] AND [Complete [Homework Assignment], [Write Exam]”

(OR refinment)

 A goal is decomposed into alternative tasks, one of which suffices for the fulfillment of the parent goal
  • “[Have Bills Paid] is OR-refined to [Pay Bills Online], [Pay Bills at the Bank]”
  • “[Be at the Appointment in Time] is OR-refined to [Walk to Appointment], [Drive to Appointment], [Take Taxi to Appointment]”

(non-deterministic effect link)

 The origin of the link, a task, brings about, upon its attempt, one of the effects of an effect group
  • “[Call Clinic to Talk with Doctor] affects (non-deterministicaly) [Spoke with Doctor], [Spoke with Doctor's Assistant], [No-one Answered]”
  • “[Applied for Loan] affects (non-deterministicaly) [Application Approved], [Application Conditionally Approved], [Application Rejected]”

(deterministic effect link)

 The origin of the link, a task, brings about, upon its attempt, a specific effect
  • “[All Money from the Account Successfully Withdrawn] affects (deterministicaly) [Account is Empty]”
  • “[Document Successfully Shredded] affects (deterministicaly) [Document Impossible to Copy]”

(positive contribution)

 Satisfaction of the origin positively affects the satisfaction of the destination
  • “[Study for Exam] positively contributes to [Increase Chances of Success in Exam]”
  • “[Item Restocked] positively contributes to [Avoid Out-of-Stock Risk]”
  • “[Avoid Out-of-Stock Risk] positively contributes to [Increase Revenues]”

(negative contribution)

 Satisfaction of the origin negativelly affects the satisfaction of the destination
  • “[Reduce Technical Support Staff] negatively contributes to [Keep Customers Happy]”
  • “[Increase Monetary Supply] negatively contributes to [Avoid Risk of Inflation]”

("plain" contribution)

Satisfaction of the origin affects the satisfaction of the destination (in an unknown or complicated way)
  • “[Provide more product options] contributes to [Keep Customers Happy]”
  • “[Reduce Personnel] contributes to [Increase Profit]”

(precedence link) 		A task cannot be performed unless the origin goal is satisfied or the origin task is performed.
  • “[Depart from origin] precedes [arrive at destination]”
  • “[Have Ingredients Acquired] precedes [Cook Food]”
